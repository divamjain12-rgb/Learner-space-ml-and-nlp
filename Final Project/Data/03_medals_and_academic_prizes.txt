1. PRESIDENT OF INDIA MEDAL (PGM)
Awarded to the B.S./B.Tech.(with honours) or B.Tech.+M.Tech. Dual Degree/IDDDP (with honours) student with the highest CPI at the end of the 4th year, based only on mandatory credit requirements, calculated after final course re-tagging. For IDDDP, awarded in the parent department. Eligibility requires overall CPI (including honours) of 9.0 or above. Ties are broken using CPI across all courses (including additional/minor courses) up to end of 4th year.
Disqualifying conditions for any medal: a DX/FF/FR grade in any course (any tag); an extension for the final-stage M.Tech Project (MTP); completing the programme beyond its prescribed duration; or any DAC/ADAC disciplinary punishment during the programme. Students who did courses via NPTEL/SWAYAM/Semester Exchange remain eligible for medals.

2. INSTITUTE GOLD MEDAL (IGM)
Awarded to the most outstanding student of the "other" track in the same year the President of India Medal is given to a B.Tech./B.S. (with honours) student, or vice versa for Dual Degree/IDDDP students. Discontinued for batches from 2022-23 onwards (batches through 2021-22 remain eligible). Eligibility and disqualification rules mirror the PGM (overall CPI including honours >= 9.0; same disqualifying conditions).

3. INSTITUTE SILVER MEDAL (ISM)
Awarded per academic unit across B.Tech.(honours), Dual Degree, B.S.(honours), B.Des., M.Sc., M.Tech., MPP, M.Des., MBA, MS/MA by Research, and LASE programmes, to students who complete all credit and honours requirements. Minimum overall CPI of 9.0 required (or CPI+honours CPI >= 9.0 for B.Tech./B.S.). For Masters/Dual-Degree/IDDDP programmes, a department normally needs 30+ graduating students to get its own Silver Medal; smaller departments are clubbed together, with two medals awarded if the clubbed graduating strength reaches 40+. Ties result in the medal being shared among tied students, counted as one medal. Same disqualification rules apply as for PGM/IGM. Exit degrees (e.g., BSc Engg. from B.Tech., M.Tech./MPP exit from a Dual PhD track) are not eligible. M.Eng./MDP/EMBA programmes are not eligible for any Institute medal.

4. DR. SHANKAR DAYAL SHARMA GOLD MEDAL
Awarded to the most outstanding graduating student overall, considering general proficiency, academic performance, extracurricular activities, and social service; eligibility is based on overall CPI. Same disqualification rules as other medals apply.

5. DONOR'S MEDALS (examples)
Several donor-funded medals exist for specific departments/programmes, generally tracking the Institute Silver Medal criteria for that department (e.g., Computer Science & Engineering, Civil Engineering) or the Shankar Dayal Sharma Gold Medal criteria (e.g., Sharad Maloo Gold Medal). The Hindi Vidya Bhavan Gold Medal goes to the highest-CPI student in the MBA (SJMSOM) programme; the Abhijeet Banerjee SJMSOM Silver Medal goes to the 2nd-highest CPI MBA student.

6. ACADEMIC PRIZES FOR ON-ROLL STUDENTS
Based on weighted SPI average (weighted by semester credits) of the previous academic year:
- 2nd-year UG students (JEE-admitted): top 20 by weighted SPI average get prizes (1st prize Rs 3000, 2nd prize Rs 2000, additional prize Rs 2000).
- 3rd/4th-year UG, B.Des. (2nd-4th year), and 5th-year Dual Degree: top 2 by weighted SPI average get prizes (1st Rs 3000, 2nd Rs 2000; if class strength is below 25, only 1st prize is given).
- 2nd-year PG programmes (M.Sc., M.Tech., M.Des., M.Phil, MBA, MA/MS by Research, MPP): same structure as above (top 2, Rs 3000/Rs 2000).
Minimum weighted SPI average of 9.0 is required to be eligible; academic unit must have a class strength of at least 5 students. Various dual-degree conversion programmes remain eligible for these prizes.
